Yala

Yala combines a strict nature reserve with a national park. Divided into 5 blocks, the park has a protected area of nearly 130,000 hectares of land consisting of light forests, scrubs, grasslands, tanks and lagoons. Two blocks are currently opened to the public. Situated in Sri Lanka’s south-east hugging the panoramic Indian Ocean, Yala was designated a wildlife sanctuary in 1900 and was designated a national park in 1938. Ironically, the park was initially used as a hunting ground for the elite under British rule. Yala is home to 44 varieties of mammal and 215 bird species. Among its more famous residents are the world’s biggest concentration of leopards, majestic elephants, sloth bears, sambars, jackals, spotted dear, peacocks, and crocodiles. The best time to visit Yala is between February and July when the water levels of the park are quite low, bringing animals into the open.

Kandy

Nestled in the lush central hills, Kandy is a cultural gem and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Known for the sacred Temple of the Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa), it is a spiritual and historical hub. Travelers can enjoy the scenic Kandy Lake, explore the Royal Botanical Gardens in Peradeniya, and witness the vibrant Esala Perahera festival. With its cool climate, rich traditions, and stunning landscapes, Kandy is a must-visit destination for those seeking history, nature, and Sri Lankan charm.

Colombo

The vibrant capital city of Sri Lanka, Colombo is a bustling metropolis that blends colonial charm with modern attractions. Visitors can explore historic landmarks like the Galle Face Green, Gangaramaya Temple, and the Independence Memorial Hall. The city offers a mix of luxury shopping malls, lively street markets, and delicious Sri Lankan cuisine. With its coastal location, scenic sunsets, and energetic nightlife, Colombo is the perfect gateway to experience the island’s rich culture and urban lifestyle.

Ella

A scenic hill-country paradise, Ella is famous for its breathtaking landscapes, lush tea plantations, and outdoor adventures. Travelers can hike to Little Adam’s Peak for stunning views, visit the iconic Nine Arches Bridge, and explore the mystical Ravana Falls. The town has a laid-back vibe with cozy cafés and panoramic viewpoints, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers looking to experience Sri Lanka’s serene beauty.
